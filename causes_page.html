Causes and Effects

,

The causes of sun burn and sun damage is based on the UV light from the sun. When a person isn't wearing sunscreen or using any other form of sun protection, UV light could cause severe and irreversible damage. It penetrates deep to a point where it can change the person's DNA. Since this is at a cellular level, the damage will not present instantly. It takes some time for physical symptoms to appear, and by then it cannot be reversed.

How do different types of UV light affect the body?

,

UVA penetrates through all layers of the skin. UVA Light is a form of UV radiation. Its longer wavelength is what allows it to penetrate through all layers of the skin. Repeated UVA exposure causes the skin to age faster. UVA exposure is commonly associated with tans and sunburns in certain individuals. Thus, there is no such thing as a safe tan. UVA Light affects the collagen, which provides the structure, strength and support to one's skin. Cappilaries which are a type of vessels are also damaged by these rays, and they are needed to transport oxygen and nutrients throughout the body. Finally, elastin fibres are damaged, which is responsible for maintaining the skin's elasticity. Overall, the DNA of a person can be altered. Since the UVA radiation hits the skin, it changes the DNA which causes mutation and defects. This could create cancerous cells, and are therefore dangerous. In sunscreen, broad-spectrum protection is what they use to associate UVA radiation, and to protect you from it.

UVB only penetrates the outermost layer of the skin, but may cause more damage. UVB light, known for its shorter wavelength, is primarily responsible for irritating the outer layer of the skin. Its high energy level leads to the formation of precancerous cells and is commonly associated with skin burning. Due to its significant impact on skin health, UVB protection is a critical component in sunscreens, indicated by the Sun Protection Factor (SPF). The SPF number provides a guideline for how long it would take for the sun's radiation to redden your skin with the product applied, compared to without it. UVB radiation poses a threat to skin health, with its effects being most powerful from mid-morning until mid-afternoon. Its intensity increases at higher altitudes and when reflected off surfaces like snow or ice. However, UVB rays can be filtered and do not penetrate glass, offering some protection when indoors.

What are the effects of UV Exposure?

,

The sun's rays causes damage to the skin's cells; and this damage is not immediately apparent upon looking at the skin. With enough exposure, our skin cells can change shape, multiply faster, and become cancerous.
