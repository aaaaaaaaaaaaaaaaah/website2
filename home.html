What is it?

,

Sunburn and sun damage result from excessive UV exposure, overwhelming the protective effects of melanin in the skin and leading to an inflammatory reaction. This battle between melanin and the sun's harmful rays can cause immediate symptoms like redness and pain, as well as long-term effects such as premature aging and increased skin cancer risk. It underscores the importance of using sun protection to prevent damage and maintain skin health.

How to prevent it?

,

Many people need to remember to apply sunscreen regularly. Approximately 80% of your exposure to the sun occurs while you’re outside performing regular everyday activities. Therefore, you must wear sunscreen all the time. Besides this, the clothing we wear will also impact the amount of UV protection our skin receives. You should also try to limit your direct exposure to sunlight between 10 am and 4 pm when the sun’s rays are the strongest. In addition to this, try seeking shade whenever possible under a tree or umbrella if you are outside your home or any building. Regularly applying lip balm also helps to protect your lips from drying out due to the sun’s harmful UV rays. Last but not least consult your doctor if any of the medications you’re taking will make your skin more sensitive to sun exposure and take any additional precautions that may be necessary for the treatment.
